Indian Nationalist

Subhas Chandra Bose (born c. January 23, 1897, Cuttack, Orissa [now Odisha], Indiaâ€”died August 18, 1945, Taipei, Taiwan) was an Indian revolutionary prominent in the independence movement against British rule of India. He also led an Indian national force from abroad against the Western powers during World War II. He was a contemporary of Mohandas K. Gandhi, at times an ally and at other times an adversary. Bose was known in particular for his militant approach to independence and for his push for socialist policies.



About The Netaji

  • Involvement in Indian National Congress (INC):
    • He Joined the INC and rose rapidly in its ranks. Became the President of the INC in 1938 and 1939. Advocated for complete and immediate independence from British rule, which put him at odds with more moderate leaders like Mahatma Gandhi.

  • Differences with Gandhi and Formation of Forward Bloc:
    • Disagreed with Gandhi's non-violent approach and slow pace of the independence movement. Resigned from the INC presidency and formed the Forward Bloc in 1939 to consolidate the anti-British forces.

  • World War II and Escape to Germany:
    • Under house arrest by the British, he escaped India in 1941. Traveled to Germany, met with Hitler, and sought support for India's independence. Made broadcasts from Berlin to encourage Indians to fight against the British.

  • Indian National Army (INA):
    • Traveled to Japan in 1943 where he took leadership of the Indian National Army (INA) formed by Indian POWs and expatriates. The INA, with Japanese support, fought against the British in Burma and Northeastern India. Famously rallied the INA with the slogan "Give me blood, and I shall give you freedom!"

  • Role in Indian Independence Movement:
    • Although the INA was eventually defeated, Bose's efforts significantly boosted Indian nationalistic fervor. His military actions and leadership inspired many Indians and contributed to the momentum leading to independence.

  • Mysterious Disappearance and Legacy:
    • Allegedly died in a plane crash in Taiwan on August 18, 1945, though there are various conspiracy theories and lack of definitive proof regarding his death. Remembered as a brave and dynamic leader, Bose's legacy is commemorated in India through various memorials, institutions, and his continued veneration as a nationalist hero.

  • Impact and Recognition:
    • His approach and methods highlighted the diversity within the Indian independence movement. Posthumously honored and celebrated in India for his unyielding dedication to the cause of freedom.
